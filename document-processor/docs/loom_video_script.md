# Loom Video Script: Document Processor Demo
## 90–100 Seconds | Personalized for Each Client

**Purpose:** A short, high-impact demo that feels like it was recorded exclusively for the individual client watching.

---

## Pre-Recording Setup

### Personalization Variables (fill before each recording)
- **[Client Name]** — e.g., "Sarah" or "the Acme team"
- **[Company Name]** — e.g., "Acme Corp" or leave generic
- **[Use Case]** — e.g., "contracts," "RFPs," "research," or "your documents"

### Technical Prep
- [ ] App loaded at: `https://document-processor-crimson-dream-2102.fly.dev` (or your deployed URL)
- [ ] Logged in with a demo account
- [ ] 2–3 sample PDFs already uploaded and processed (status: COMPLETED)
- [ ] Browser zoom at 100%, window maximized
- [ ] Close extra tabs; hide bookmarks bar if possible
- [ ] Loom set to record screen + camera (optional but recommended for personalization)

---

## Script by Timestamp

### 0:00–0:08 | HOOK (8 sec)
**Screen:** App homepage — main header visible, clean view.

**What to say (warm, direct, eye contact if on camera):**
> "Hey [Client Name], I put together a quick walkthrough specifically for you. I know you're busy, so this is under two minutes — I'll show you exactly how this works for [use case]."

**How to say it:**
- Slightly slower than normal; pause after "specifically for you"
- Smile; make it feel like a 1:1 call, not a generic pitch

---

### 0:08–0:22 | UPLOAD & AI SUMMARIZATION (14 sec)
**Screen:** Click **"Upload & Process"** tab. Show the upload area and the summarization prompt field.

**What to say:**
> "First — upload. You drag in your PDFs, and here's what makes it different: you can tell the AI exactly what you want. For example, 'Summarize the key risks for executives' or 'Extract the main obligations.' Same document, different summaries depending on who needs it."

**What to show:**
- 0:08 — Click "Upload & Process" tab
- 0:12 — Hover over file uploader, point at summarization prompt
- 0:16 — Type a short example prompt (e.g., "List the top 3 action items") — don't need to submit
- 0:20 — Scroll to show "Your Documents" list with at least one COMPLETED doc

**How to say it:**
- Emphasize "exactly what you want" and "different summaries"
- Keep pace steady; avoid rushing

---

### 0:22–0:38 | DOCUMENT LIST & AI SUMMARY (16 sec)
**Screen:** Documents list with at least one completed document. Expand the AI Summary for one doc.

**What to say:**
> "Once it's done, you see everything here. Each document gets an AI summary based on your prompt — you can expand it, copy it, or download it. No more digging through 50-page PDFs to find the one thing you need."

**What to show:**
- 0:22 — Scroll so a completed document card is clearly visible
- 0:26 — Click "AI Summary" expander to reveal the summary
- 0:30 — Briefly highlight the summary text (don't read it aloud)
- 0:34 — Point to "Download Summary" button
- 0:36 — Hover over "Chat" button

**How to say it:**
- "No more digging" — slightly emphatic, like you get their pain
- Confident, not salesy

---

### 0:38–0:62 | CHAT WITH DOCUMENTS (24 sec)
**Screen:** Switch to "Chat with Data" tab. Show document selector, then ask a question and show the answer with sources.

**What to say:**
> "The real power is here — chat. You can ask questions in plain English. 'What are the payment terms?' 'Summarize the key findings.' You can search one document or across all of them. Every answer shows you exactly where it came from — which document, which section — so you can verify it yourself."

**What to show:**
- 0:38 — Click "Chat with Data" tab
- 0:42 — Point to document selector: "Search All Documents" vs single doc
- 0:46 — Type a question (e.g., "What are the main points?") and hit Enter
- 0:50 — Wait for answer to load (2–3 sec)
- 0:54 — Scroll to show the answer
- 0:58 — Expand "Sources" to show document + chunk references
- 0:60 — Briefly point at similarity score or filename

**How to say it:**
- "The real power" — slight pause before "chat"
- "Exactly where it came from" — emphasize trust/verification
- If answer loads slowly, fill with: "It's pulling from your actual documents, not making things up."

---

### 0:62–0:92 | CLOSE & CTA (30 sec)
**Screen:** Back to main view or chat tab. Clean, professional frame.

**What to say:**
> "That's it — upload, get AI summaries your way, and chat with your data. Built for [use case], but it works for contracts, RFPs, research, whatever you throw at it. I'd love to hear what you think or set up a quick call to tailor this for [Company Name]. Link's in the description. Thanks for watching."

**What to show:**
- 0:62 — Return to a clean view (documents or chat)
- 0:70 — Optional: show sidebar with "My Documents" count
- 0:75 — Keep screen static; focus on camera if recording face
- 0:85 — Smile, wrap up

**How to say it:**
- "I'd love to hear" — genuine, not pushy
- "Thanks for watching" — warm sign-off
- Pause 1–2 sec at end before stopping recording

## Personalization Tips

1. **Per-client tweaks**
   - Legal: emphasize "contracts," "obligations," "clauses"
   - Procurement: "RFPs," "requirements," "compliance"
   - Executives: "key findings," "risks," "decisions"
   - Research: "summarize," "compare," "extract"

2. **Use-case examples**
   - Replace "Summarize the key risks" with something from their industry
   - Mention a doc type they use (e.g., "your SOWs," "your NDAs")

3. **Name drops**
   - Say their name 1–2 times (opening, closing)
   - Mention their company in the CTA

4. **Tone**
   - Consultant: confident, efficient
   - Sales: warm, benefit-focused
   - Technical: precise, feature-aware
